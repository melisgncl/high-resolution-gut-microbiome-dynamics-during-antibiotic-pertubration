## Spectinomycin binding context (cross-species)
  Spectinomycin binds between helix h34 of 16S rRNA and the neck of the 30S subunit.
  The N-terminal beta-hairpin of ribosomal protein S5 (uS5, rpsE) contacts h34 at
  this interface. This contact region is structurally conserved across bacteria.

  In E. coli K12, rpsE N-terminal loop (positions 19–26):
    N19-R20-V21-S22-K23-T24-V25-K26  (NP_417762.1)

  Published spectinomycin resistance mutations in this loop:
    - Bilgin et al. 1990 (EMBO J 9:735): rpsE mutations in E. coli N-terminal domain
      (positions ~20-26) confer spectinomycin resistance.
    - Clinically observed in N. gonorrhoeae (Unemo et al. 2013): K20E, T23I, K24N in the
      N. gonorrhoeae rpsE N-terminal loop confer high-level spectinomycin resistance.
    - Carter et al. 2000 (Science 292:883): 30S crystal structure places S5 N-terminal
      domain in contact with h34 at the spectinomycin binding pocket.

  The E. coli data establish that the K20–K26 zone of rpsE is the spectinomycin-contact
  region. The comparison to E. coli here is a coordinate reference only — structural
  consequences are those occurring in Paenibacillus rpsE.

  the region equivalent to E. coli S22-K23-T24 — squarely within the spectinomycin-

    - This loop in I6 contains K22 (Lysine) — a positively charged residue homologous to
